# Blurting Out the Plot
## in which we don't quote know what to do with this little life so we just name the player "Lior"
### in which we also make Lior a boy, temporarily, because i need to actually understand what those people want


Lior is your average 17 year old in secondary school. An average school, average grades, average job, average life, average family, average everything. Sometimes he's fine with that, you see.

*Is he really?*

Yes, he is, because Lior has always been fine with everything. He will continue to be fine with everything, because he is Lior. And in all of his averageness, they tell him not to have ambition in anything. It's not said in words, but in the glances his classmates make when Lior's face is colored by the slightest hint of disappointment, his own eyes raking over his ranking: 253/506. In the soft sigh that leaves his mother's lips every time she glances at her middle child, never uttering a single word as she knits sweaters for her eldest or cooks meals for her youngest.

He isn't unloved. He knows it. It's just a fact---a fact that he is the middle of everything.

It's in Lior's name: *or*. Not *the*, not *a*, not even *and*, but *or*.

All he's ever wanted in life was to be noticed by someone. That's not too much to ask, is it? in between the gaps in his memory and the dullness of his everyday life, he can't remember a single moment he was noticed.

So when a traveling circus, gilded and great, comes by to Lior's hometown, he finds himself shooting his shot: in a meeting with a mysterious masked man in the back, with eyes that glitter just a bit too much in the dimmed lights. His finger brushes against theirs, once! And he finds life there. Aphantasia-like symptoms consume him. The man in the circus doesn't initially intend on meeting Lior; not until Lior forces himself into a meeting meant for someone else.
